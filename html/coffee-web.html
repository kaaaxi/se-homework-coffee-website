about Coffee bean

Colombia

Colombian coffee beans are known for their medium roast and balanced taste. It has subtle nutty and fresh herbal flavors with moderate acidity and alcohol. Enjoy with milk and chocolate or nuts. The story of this coffee bean tells of an adventurous journey, climbing mountains, wading water and traversing jungles just to taste a cup of Colombian coffee. The spirit of adventure it presents - the fearless courage in the face of difficulties - is a quality we need to learn in life.

Kenya

Kenyan coffee beans are known for their distinctive grapefruit and blackcurrant flavors, which are particularly prominent when refrigerated. The acidity of this coffee bean is high, and the alcohol is noble. It is recommended with grapefruit and berries. With its refreshing acidity and wine-like texture, Kenyan coffee is often found in many Summer seasonal blends, such as KatiKati and Summer Breeze.

Antigua, Guatemala

Guatemalan Antigua coffee beans are appreciated for their elegant and delicate flavor, cocoa and soft spice notes, as well as slight lemon notes. Both acidity and alcohol are moderate. It is recommended to serve with French Passion chocolate cake or cinnamon sugar apple. The story of this coffee bean underscores the irreplaceability of Antigua, whether as a cup of coffee, something to hold on to, or a language. Its fragrance drips down and penetrates the soul.

Ethiopia

Ethiopian coffee beans are known for their light dark chocolate, sweet orange flavor and a hint of green pepper spicy aftertaste. High acidity, moderate alcohol. It is recommended to serve with classic Black Forest cake or French Passion chocolate cake. This coffee bean, with its unique flavor and high-quality taste, has become a favorite of coffee lovers.

Americano

Americano, like a simple jazz, does not need gorgeous decoration, it can show the purest charm of coffee beans. Each bite is a tribute to the essence of coffee, fresh without losing depth, simple yet full of character. It is not only a drink, but also a reflection of life attitude - simple but not simple, ordinary to see the extraordinary

Caramel macchiato

mocha

Mocha's reputation stems from its rich flavor, appealing presentation, versatility, and emotional significance. These qualities solidify its cherished status among coffee enthusiasts. As both a sensory delight and a comforting presence, mocha remains a vital part of coffee culture, captivating palates and hearts with its distinct allure.

Flat white

The main characteristics of the taste are rich coffee aroma, silky milk foam, balanced sour and sweet degree, rich taste level and moderate coffee concentration. These characteristics make flat white become a favorite coffee drink for many people, whether it is coffee lovers or ordinary consumers, they can find their favorite taste in the process of tasting flat white.

Matcha latte

Matcha latte, like a touch of fresh green on a spring day, blends Oriental tea charm with Western milk perfectly. Its delicate foam moves gently on the tip of the tongue, like the cherry petals in the spring breeze, light without losing depth. Each bite of the matcha latte is a taste trip, taking you through the teahouses of Kyoto to experience the tranquility and harmony.
